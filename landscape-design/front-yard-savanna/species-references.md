## Chapter sections that ground the design

### Chapter 02 — Ecoregions & Growing Conditions

[docs/chapters/02-ecoregions-growing-conditions/index.md](../../docs/chapters/02-ecoregions-growing-conditions/index.md)

- **Lines 193-209: "Prairie-Forest Border"** — defines oak savanna as fire-maintained open woodland with widely spaced bur oaks and prairie understory; explicitly the pre-settlement community of this site.
- **Line 391: "sandy soils support dry prairie and oak savanna communities"** — soil-community linkage relevant to Hayden / Lester loam expected on this parcel.

### Chapter 03 — Prairie Plants & Grasslands

[docs/chapters/03-prairie-plants-grasslands/index.md](../../docs/chapters/03-prairie-plants-grasslands/index.md)

- Tallgrass prairie ecosystem, fire ecology, grass root systems (6-10 ft deep). Grounds the choice of Bluestem and Dropseed as connective tissue.

### Chapter 04 — Woodland & Forest Plants

[docs/chapters/04-woodland-forest-plants/index.md](../../docs/chapters/04-woodland-forest-plants/index.md)

- **Lines 15-26: oak savannas as fire-maintained woodlands at the prairie-forest border** — locates the design within the broader forest-layer discussion.

### Chapter 05 — Wetland & Shoreline Plants

[docs/chapters/05-wetland-shoreline-plants/index.md](../../docs/chapters/05-wetland-shoreline-plants/index.md)

- Plant adaptations to wet feet (aerenchyma); marsh species inventory. Grounds the swale's wet-zone palette (Iris, Cardinal Flower, sedges, rushes).

### Chapter 06 — Pollinators & Wildlife

[docs/chapters/06-pollinators-wildlife/index.md](../../docs/chapters/06-pollinators-wildlife/index.md)

- **Lines 13-206:** 400+ native bee species in MN; bumblebees, mining bees, leafcutter bees, Mason bees.
- **Monarch lifecycle:** milkweeds for eggs (Butterfly Milkweed in Zone 3, Swamp Milkweed in swale = two host species, two habitats), specific plants for adult nectar.
- **Threats to pollinators:** habitat loss, pesticides, climate mismatch — argues for the no-pesticide commitment and continuous-bloom approach.

### Chapter 10 — Garden Design with Native Plants ★ (most-cited)

[docs/chapters/10-garden-design-native-plants/index.md](../../docs/chapters/10-garden-design-native-plants/index.md)

- **Lines 66-80: sunlight categories** — south-facing >6 hr = full sun; full prairie-savanna palette in scope.
- **Lines 131-143: microclimates** — south-facing wall radiates warmth, effectively raising hardiness by half a zone (4b → 5a microclimate); rain shadows under roof overhangs; wet spots near downspouts. **All three apply directly here.**
- **Lines 374-413: Rain Garden** — three-zone design (wettest center, moist slopes, dry rim) at 10 ft setback from foundation. **The vegetated swale follows this template.**
- **Lines 415-445: Pollinator Garden Design** — continuous bloom April-October, diverse flower shapes, larval host plants, nesting habitat, no pesticides. **All five principles drive species selection here.**
- **Line 583: bloom time as plant-selection criterion** — ties directly to the bloom-calendar table in plan.md.

### Chapter glossary candidates

If readers click through from the design package back to the book, these glossary terms anchor the explanation:

- **Oak savanna** — fire-maintained open woodland with widely spaced trees (typically bur oak) and prairie/grassland understory
- **Microclimate** — local variation in temperature, moisture, wind, or light that differs from the surrounding regional climate; created by structures, slopes, water, vegetation
- **Bioswale / vegetated swale** — a shallow planted depression that conveys and infiltrates stormwater; uses dense fibrous-rooted plants (sedges, rushes, prairie grasses) to slow flow and prevent erosion without rock armoring
- **Host plant** — a specific plant species required by a butterfly or moth larva for development (e.g., milkweeds for Monarch caterpillars, lupine for Karner Blue)
- **Pre-settlement vegetation** — the plant communities present in Minnesota before European-American settlement, mapped by Francis Marschner from Public Land Survey notes
- **Provenance** — the geographic origin of plant seed or stock; locally-adapted MN provenance is preferred for ecological function and hardiness

## How to re-render the design overlays

If you want to nudge marker positions, swap species, or update the bloom calendar, edit the constants near the top of `annotate.py` and re-run:

```bash
cd landscape-design/front-yard-savanna
python3 annotate.py
```

Three images regenerate in `annotated/`.

## Site-walkthrough talking points (use during their visit)

When they arrive, walk them around the bed in this order:

1. **Stand at the bay window first.** Show them the *indoor sightline*. The central bed has to stay below the window sill. This is non-negotiable.

2. **Walk to the west end.** This is where the Serviceberry goes. Point out:
   - The bed expands ~12 ft into the lawn here for the bench pocket
   - Afternoon shade is the goal under the serviceberry canopy
   - The bench faces back toward the entry

3. **Walk along the front of the bed.** Show them:
   - Where the path enters (east end, off the existing concrete walkway)
   - How the path threads across to the bench
   - That the bed front edge is *soft* — no steel, possibly a fieldstone mowing strip

4. **Walk to the east end / front porch corner.** Show them:
   - Two existing arborvitae stay
   - Existing crabapple stays (front entry)
   - Where the bed terminates at the arborvitae

5. **Walk to the side walkway.** Show them:
   - The white river-rock area
   - Tell them: **Rima does not want rock.** Vegetated bioswale only.
   - HVAC condenser and gas meter on west wall — 3 ft service clearance required
   - Side service door on the west side

6. **Step back to the lawn.** Talk about:
   - Mugo removal (mini-excavator? hand removal?)
   - Construction access (driveway? front lawn?)
   - Soil disposal
   - Timeline: when can they start, how long does install take

## Questions to ask EVERY installer

Take the booklet's notes pages and write their answers. Ask all three the same questions for apples-to-apples comparison.

### Experience

1. How many oak-savanna or prairie-style residential installs have you done in the past 3 years?
2. Can you show me 2-3 references — gardens you can drive me past or homeowners I can call?
3. Have you converted river-rock drainage swales to vegetated bioswales before? (If they say "we do dry creeks all the time" — different thing. Push for fibrous-rooted plant matrix experience.)

### Removal

4. How will you remove the mature mugo pines? Mini-excavator? Hand?
5. What's the disposal cost / process? (Mugo root mass is large.)
6. Do you handle the creeping charlie pre-removal, or is that on us?
7. Will you test-pit the swale before full removal to confirm what's underneath (drain tile vs. fabric vs. just gravel)?

### Plants & sources

8. Where do you source your plants? Specifically — are they MN-provenance, or generic nursery stock from somewhere warmer?
9. Will you guarantee Prairie Moon Nursery, Outback Nursery, or Glacial Ridge Growers as sources for the species we've specified?
10. What size containers do you install? (1-gal for forbs is right; 1-quart for some plugs; B&B for the Serviceberry.)
11. Will you install the Serviceberry at 6-8 ft B&B size, not bare-root or container?

### Hardscape

12. What's your stepping-stone source — natural-cut Chilton limestone or equivalent? Show me a sample.
13. How do you set steppers — screenings or mortared? (Screenings is what we want.)
14. What edge treatment do you recommend — trench-cut or fieldstone mowing strip?
15. Do you have a bench-pad spec, or do we need to provide one?

### Bioswale

16. Walk me through how you'd plant the swale channel. (Listen for: dense plug spacing, biodegradable erosion blanket, sedge/rush armor matrix.)
17. What's your year-1 protection plan if a heavy storm hits before sedges root?
18. Have you used erosion-control blankets before? Which brand?

### Warranty & care

19. What's your warranty on woody plants (Serviceberry, Ninebarks, Gray Dogwood)?
20. What about forbs and grasses? Most native specialists guarantee year-1 establishment, not season-1.
21. Do you offer a year-1 maintenance contract, or is it on us?
22. When do you recommend we install — spring (April-May) or fall (Sep-Oct)?

### Schedule & cost

23. When can you start? Lead time?
24. Single-season install, or phased?
25. Quote breakdown — separate line items for: removal, hardscape, plants, install labor, warranty, maintenance?
26. What's *not* included? (Watering year 1? Mulch top-up? Spring weed pull?)

## Red flags

If you hear any of these, slow down:

- **"We can do this with junipers and daylilies, those are native."** — daylilies are not native; junipers are not local-genotype. Walk away.
- **"Rock is just easier for the swale."** — they haven't done a vegetated bioswale before.
- **"We'll spray Roundup before planting."** — pre-emergent is fine; broad-spectrum spray on a bed about to host pollinators is not.
- **"Yeah we can get those plants from Bachman's."** — Bachman's is a great garden center but doesn't carry the MN-provenance native stock you want for ecological function. Push for Prairie Moon / Outback / Glacial Ridge.
- **"You don't need a bench pad, just set it on the lawn."** — settles, tilts, kills grass under it. Walk back through Detail 2 of the booklet.
- **"This is overkill for a residential project."** — they don't get it. Move on.
- **No written warranty.** — never sign without it.

## Green flags

- They ask about the soil before quoting (Hayden / Lester / Kilkenny loam — they should care).
- They ask if you've already done the Web Soil Survey lookup.
- They look at the bay-window sightline constraint and nod.
- They mention specific species correctly — pronounce *Schizachyrium*, know *Carex stricta* vs. *Carex vulpinoidea*.
- They have opinions on Serviceberry cultivars vs. straight species.
- They know what the Marschner map is.
- They've been to the Arboretum's prairie garden.

## After the visits

Use the notes pages in the booklet to capture each visit. After all three:

1. Compare quotes line-by-line (removal, hardscape, plants, labor, warranty)
2. Compare warranty terms — sometimes a $1,500 difference is one firm's missing year-1 plant guarantee
3. Call references for each finalist
4. Pick the one whose answers gave you the most confidence on the bioswale specifically — that's the hardest part of this project

## Sample timeline

If you sign a contract by mid-June:

| Date | Milestone |
|---|---|
| Jul: site work | Mugo removal, swale prep, path laid, bench pad |
| Aug: planting (early) | Best time for spring-planted plugs to root before fall |
| Aug-Sep: establishment watering | First six weeks critical |
| Oct: erosion blanket goes on swale | Before fall storms |
| Nov-Mar: rest | Plants dormant; soak ground occasionally during thaws |
| Apr year-1: spring care | Cut back grasses, weed inspection, light pruning |
| May year-1: first bloom | Lupine, Prairie Smoke, Baptisia. Take photos. |

If you instead aim for **fall planting** (preferred for Serviceberry and many woodies):

| Date | Milestone |
|---|---|
| Aug: site work | Removal, hardscape |
| Sep-early Oct: planting | Cool soil, reliable rain, root establishment over winter |
| Oct: erosion blanket | Before winter |
| Nov-Mar: rest |  |
| Apr year-1: emerge | Strongest first-season because roots had a head start |

Fall planting is the professional preference for this type of project. Confirm timeline with whoever you pick.

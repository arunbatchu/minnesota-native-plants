## Overview

**Minnesota Native Plants** is a free, open-access intelligent textbook suitable for use in college courses, community education programs, Master Gardener training, nature center workshops, and self-directed study. The content covers 289 concepts across 17 chapters, progressing from foundational ecology to capstone projects.

## Course Structure Options

### Full Semester (15–16 Weeks)

Cover all 17 chapters at roughly one chapter per week, with additional time for the capstone project in Chapter 17.

| Weeks | Chapters | Theme |
|-------|----------|-------|
| 1–2 | 1–2 | Ecological foundations and ecoregions |
| 3–5 | 3–5 | Plant communities: prairie, woodland, wetland |
| 6–7 | 6–7 | Pollinators and plant identification |
| 8–9 | 8–9 | Invasive species ID and removal |
| 10–11 | 10–11 | Garden design and planting practices |
| 12–13 | 12–14 | Restoration, cultural uses, resources |
| 14 | 15–16 | Systems thinking and critical thinking |
| 15–16 | 17 | Capstone projects |

### Short Course (6–8 Weeks)

Focus on core chapters for a condensed experience:

- Chapters 1–2 (foundations)
- Chapters 3 or 4 (one plant community)
- Chapter 6 (pollinators)
- Chapter 8 (invasive species)
- Chapter 10 (garden design)
- Chapter 15 (systems thinking)

### Workshop Format (1–2 Days)

Select 3–4 chapters relevant to your audience. Pair each chapter with its MicroSim for hands-on engagement.

## Using the Interactive Features

### MicroSims

Each MicroSim is a self-contained p5.js simulation embedded via iframe. They work on any device with a modern browser. Recommended uses:

- **In-class demonstrations** — project the simulation and explore it as a group
- **Lab activities** — have students work through simulations individually and record observations
- **Homework** — assign specific simulations with guided questions

### Quizzes

Each chapter includes a 10-question multiple-choice quiz. Questions are distributed across Bloom's Taxonomy levels:

- **Remember/Understand** — recall and comprehension questions
- **Apply/Analyze** — scenario-based questions requiring application of concepts
- **Evaluate/Create** — higher-order questions asking for judgment or synthesis

Quizzes are self-grading in the browser. Consider using them as:

- Pre-reading assessments to gauge prior knowledge
- Post-reading comprehension checks
- Study aids before exams

### Learning Graph

The interactive learning graph visualizes how all 289 concepts connect. Use it to:

- Show students how current topics relate to what they have already learned
- Help students identify prerequisite knowledge they may need to review
- Plan custom learning paths for students with different backgrounds

## Assessment Strategies

### Formative Assessment

- Chapter quizzes (built-in)
- MicroSim observation journals
- Discussion questions based on Bree's key concept prompts
- Peer teaching of glossary terms

### Summative Assessment

- Capstone projects (Chapter 17 provides templates)
- Portfolio of MicroSim explorations with written reflections
- Native plant garden design proposal using the Garden Layout Planner
- Invasive species management plan for a local site

## Adapting for Your Audience

| Audience | Suggested Focus |
|----------|----------------|
| College biology students | All chapters, emphasis on Chapters 6, 12, 15 |
| Master Gardeners | Chapters 3–5, 8–11 |
| K-12 educators | Chapters 1, 3, 6, 10 with MicroSims |

## Technical Requirements

- Modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
- Internet connection for accessing the site and MicroSims
- No software installation required
- Works on desktop, tablet, and mobile devices

# Partridge Pea

*Chamaecrista fasciculata*



_No photos available yet. See [Learn More](#learn-more) links below._

Partridge Pea (*Chamaecrista fasciculata*) is a cheerful annual wildflower of sunny, sandy, and disturbed ground. From midsummer into fall it produces bright yellow flowers with reddish centers, set against fine, fern-like compound leaves that fold up when touched. As a legume, it fixes nitrogen and is often used in restoration seed mixes to help rebuild poor soils. It is one of the wildflowers seeded into the meadow at the Eloise Butler Wildflower Garden.

Partridge Pea is a wildlife favorite: small cup-shaped glands at the base of its leaves (extrafloral nectaries) feed ants and other beneficial insects, it is a larval host for several sulphur butterflies, and its seeds are eaten by game birds — including the partridge that gives it its name.

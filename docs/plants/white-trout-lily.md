# White Trout Lily

*Erythronium albidum*



_No photos available yet. See [Learn More](#learn-more) links below._

White Trout Lily (*Erythronium albidum*) is a spring ephemeral that forms large colonies on rich woodland floors. Its name comes from its mottled, brownish-green leaves, which resemble the markings of a brook trout. Each flowering plant sends up a single nodding white flower with petals that curve gracefully backward. Colonies can be decades or even centuries old, spreading slowly by underground runners — most of the plants in a patch are leaves only, and just a few bloom each year. It is a familiar sight in the woodland section of the Eloise Butler Wildflower Garden.

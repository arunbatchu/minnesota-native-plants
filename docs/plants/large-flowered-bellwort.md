# Large-Flowered Bellwort

*Uvularia grandiflora*



_No photos available yet. See [Learn More](#learn-more) links below._

Large-Flowered Bellwort (*Uvularia grandiflora*) is a graceful spring woodland wildflower with an unmistakable drooping habit. Its stem appears to pierce through the leaves, and from it dangle pale yellow, bell-shaped flowers with twisted petals — as if the whole plant is gently wilting even at its peak. It grows in rich, shaded woods and is one of the spring bloomers you can enjoy in the woodland garden at the Eloise Butler Wildflower Garden.

## Quick Facts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Scientific name** | *Uvularia grandiflora* |
| **Family** | Colchicaceae (autumn-crocus family) |
| **Height** | 12–18 inches (up to 30 inches in fruit) |
| **Bloom time** | April–May |
| **Sun** | Part shade to full shade |
| **Moisture** | Medium |
| **Soil** | Rich, moist woodland soil |
| **Wildlife value** | Nectar and pollen for bumblebees and other early native bees |

# References: Introduction to Native Plants and Ecology

1. [Native Plant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Native_plant) - Wikipedia - Defines native plants and explains why indigenous species are ecologically important, providing foundational context for understanding Minnesota's flora.

2. [Ecology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecology) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of ecological principles including ecosystems, species interactions, and energy flow that underpin native plant community dynamics.

3. [Flora of Minnesota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora_of_Minnesota) - Wikipedia - Catalogs the diverse plant species found across Minnesota's biomes, offering a broad survey of the state's native vegetation and biogeographic history.

4. Tallamy, Douglas W. - Bringing Nature Home - Timber Press - Explores the critical relationship between native plants and wildlife, making a compelling case for why native species support local food webs and biodiversity.

5. Kricher, John C. - The Balance of Nature: Ecology's Enduring Myth - Princeton University Press - Examines foundational ecological concepts and challenges common misconceptions, helping readers build an accurate understanding of how plant communities function.

6. [Minnesota Native Plant Sources](https://www.dnr.state.mn.us/gardens/nativeplants/index.html) - Minnesota DNR - State agency resource listing native plant species suitable for Minnesota landscapes, with guidance on identification and ecological benefits.

7. [Native Plants for Minnesota Landscapes](https://extension.umn.edu/find-plants/native-plants) - University of Minnesota Extension - Research-based guide to selecting and growing native plants in Minnesota, including species profiles and site-matching recommendations.

8. [Minnesota Wildflowers](https://www.minnesotawildflowers.info/) - MinnesotaWildflowers.info - Extensive photo-rich database of Minnesota's native wildflowers with detailed identification keys, habitat descriptions, and bloom-time information.

9. [Introduction to Ecology](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/ap-biology/ecology-ap) - Khan Academy - Free educational modules covering core ecology concepts such as populations, communities, and ecosystems relevant to understanding native plant environments.

10. [Landscaping with Native Plants](https://www.nwf.org/Garden-for-Wildlife/About/Native-Plants) - National Wildlife Federation - Provides practical guidance on using native plants to support wildlife habitat, with regional plant finders and garden design resources.

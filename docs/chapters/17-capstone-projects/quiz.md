#### 6. You are developing a Systems Map Project. Which of the following would best demonstrate systems thinking rather than linear thinking?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. A timeline showing when each prairie plant species blooms
2. A list of all native plant species found in your county
3. A diagram showing feedback loops, interconnections, and cascading effects between plants, pollinators, soil microbes, water systems, and wildlife in a specific ecosystem
4. A bar chart comparing the heights of ten native grass species
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **C**. A systems map should show the web of relationships within an ecosystem, including feedback loops (both stabilizing and amplifying), interconnections between components (plants, pollinators, soil organisms, water, wildlife), and cascading effects that demonstrate how changes in one component ripple through others. Timelines, species lists, and bar charts organize information linearly but do not capture the circular, interconnected nature of ecological systems that is the core of systems thinking.

    **Concept Tested:** Systems Map Project

#### 7. The Maintenance Calendar Project requires you to create a month-by-month maintenance plan for a native planting. What is the most important principle to apply from Chapter 11?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. Native plantings need daily watering throughout the year
2. Maintenance tasks must be timed to the plant community's seasonal needs, such as mowing before spring growth, avoiding disturbance during nesting season, and conducting prescribed burns in appropriate windows
3. All maintenance should be concentrated in a single weekend in April
4. Native plantings never require any maintenance activity
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. An effective maintenance calendar aligns tasks with seasonal ecological needs: spring weed management before natives emerge, avoiding mowing during grassland bird nesting season (May-July), timing invasive removal before seed set, conducting prescribed burns during appropriate weather windows, leaving standing plant material through winter for overwintering pollinators and bird food, and dividing perennials in spring or fall. The calendar demonstrates understanding that maintenance is an ongoing, seasonally-driven stewardship commitment.

    **Concept Tested:** Maintenance Calendar Project

#### 9. Evaluate this capstone project approach: A student selects a garden design site, skips the site assessment, chooses plants based entirely on flower color preferences, and creates a visually appealing design drawing. What fundamental problems does this approach have?

<div class="upper-alpha" markdown>
1. No problems; visual appeal is the primary goal of any garden design
2. The approach skips site assessment, so plants may not match actual sunlight, soil, and moisture conditions, leading to plant failures regardless of how attractive the design looks on paper
3. The only problem is that the student should have included more grass species
4. Flower color is the only criterion that matters for ecological function
</div>

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. Skipping site assessment is the most fundamental error in garden design. Without knowing the site's actual sunlight levels, soil type, moisture conditions, and microclimates, plant selections are essentially random guesses. A sun-loving prairie plant placed in deep shade will fail, and a wetland species in dry sand will die, regardless of how beautiful the design drawing looks. The "right plant, right place" principle from Chapter 10 requires that site conditions drive plant selection, not aesthetic preferences alone.

    **Concept Tested:** Garden Design Capstone

??? question "Show Answer"
    The correct answer is **B**. An effective, persuasive presentation combines systems thinking with evidence-based communication.

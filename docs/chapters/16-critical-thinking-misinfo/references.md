# References: Critical Thinking and Misinformation Detection

## Wikipedia

1. [Scientific Literacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_literacy) - Wikipedia - Defines the ability to understand and evaluate scientific claims, a core competency for assessing environmental information and distinguishing evidence-based ecology from pseudoscience.

2. [Media Literacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Media_literacy) - Wikipedia - Covers the skills needed to critically analyze media messages, including identifying bias, evaluating sources, and recognizing persuasion techniques in environmental reporting.

3. [Misinformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Misinformation) - Wikipedia - Explores how false or misleading information spreads, with examples from health and environmental contexts that parallel common misconceptions about native plants and ecology.

## Textbooks

4. Sagan, Carl. *The Demon-Haunted World: Science as a Candle in the Dark* - Ballantine Books - Presents a toolkit for skeptical thinking and scientific reasoning that helps readers evaluate extraordinary claims and distinguish science from pseudoscience.

5. Kahneman, Daniel. *Thinking, Fast and Slow* - Farrar, Straus and Giroux - Explains cognitive biases and heuristics that influence human judgment, providing insight into why people accept misinformation and how to counteract flawed reasoning.

## Online Resources

6. [Evaluating Information Sources](https://libguides.umn.edu/evaluatinginformation) - University of Minnesota Libraries - A practical guide for assessing the credibility, accuracy, and relevance of information sources, with specific strategies for evaluating scientific and environmental claims.

7. [Minnesota Pollution Control Agency - Environmental Data](https://www.pca.state.mn.us/data) - Minnesota PCA - Provides access to verified environmental monitoring data for Minnesota, demonstrating how to use authoritative government sources to fact-check ecological claims.

8. [News Literacy Project](https://newslit.org/) - News Literacy Project - Offers educational tools and lessons on identifying credible journalism, spotting misinformation, and applying critical thinking skills to environmental news coverage.

9. [CRAAP Test for Evaluating Sources](https://libguides.csuchico.edu/craap-test) - California State University, Chico - Presents the widely used Currency, Relevance, Authority, Accuracy, and Purpose framework for systematically evaluating the quality of any information source.

10. [Climate Misinformation and How to Counter It](https://skepticalscience.com/) - Skeptical Science - A peer-reviewed resource that catalogs common climate and environmental misconceptions and provides evidence-based rebuttals useful for ecological literacy education.

# References: Systems Thinking in Ecology

## Wikipedia

1. [Systems Ecology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_ecology) - Wikipedia - Introduces the interdisciplinary field that applies systems thinking to ecological research, covering energy flows, nutrient cycling, and ecosystem-level feedback mechanisms.

2. [Trophic Cascade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trophic_cascade) - Wikipedia - Explains how changes at one trophic level ripple through an ecosystem, illustrating the interconnected relationships between predators, herbivores, and plant communities.

3. [Feedback Loop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feedback) - Wikipedia - Defines positive and negative feedback loops with examples from biology and ecology, providing essential vocabulary for understanding ecosystem self-regulation and stability.

## Textbooks

4. Meadows, Donella H. *Thinking in Systems: A Primer* - Chelsea Green Publishing - A foundational introduction to systems thinking that explains stocks, flows, and feedback loops using accessible examples applicable to ecological and environmental systems.

5. Odum, Eugene P. and Gary W. Barrett. *Fundamentals of Ecology* - Cengage Learning - The classic ecology textbook that pioneered the ecosystem-level approach to ecology, covering energy flow, biogeochemical cycles, and community dynamics in natural systems.

## Online Resources

6. [Minnesota Biological Survey](https://www.dnr.state.mn.us/mbs/index.html) - Minnesota DNR - Documents the biodiversity and ecological health of Minnesota's native plant communities using systematic survey data that reveals ecosystem-level patterns and connections.

7. [Cedar Creek Ecosystem Science Reserve](https://www.cedarcreek.umn.edu/) - University of Minnesota - A Long Term Ecological Research site in Minnesota conducting pioneering experiments on biodiversity, ecosystem function, and how plant communities respond to environmental change.

8. [The Systems Thinker](https://thesystemsthinker.com/) - Leverage Networks - Offers articles, diagrams, and case studies that teach systems thinking tools like causal loop diagrams and behavior-over-time graphs applicable to ecological analysis.

9. [Millennium Ecosystem Assessment](https://www.millenniumassessment.org/) - United Nations - A comprehensive global assessment of ecosystem services and human well-being that demonstrates how systems thinking applies to large-scale ecological and policy challenges.

10. [Minnesota Phenology Network](https://www.usanpn.org/nn/minnesota) - USA National Phenology Network - Tracks seasonal biological events across Minnesota, illustrating feedback relationships between climate, plant flowering, pollinator activity, and ecosystem timing.

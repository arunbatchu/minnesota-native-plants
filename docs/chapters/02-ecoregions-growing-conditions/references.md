# References: Minnesota's Ecoregions and Growing Conditions

1. [Ecoregions of Minnesota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_ecoregions_in_Minnesota) - Wikipedia - Describes Minnesota's distinct ecological regions from tallgrass prairie to boreal forest, explaining how geography and climate shape plant communities statewide.

2. [Hardiness Zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hardiness_zone) - Wikipedia - Explains the USDA Plant Hardiness Zone system used to determine which plants survive winter temperatures, directly applicable to Minnesota's zones 3a through 4b.

3. [Soil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil) - Wikipedia - Covers soil formation, composition, and classification systems essential for understanding the diverse soil types that influence plant growth across Minnesota.

4. Tester, John R. - Minnesota's Natural Heritage: An Ecological Perspective - University of Minnesota Press - Definitive guide to Minnesota's ecological regions, landforms, and natural communities, providing deep context for the state's growing conditions.

5. Olis, Alan R. - Minnesota Plant Hardiness and Growing Guide - Adventure Publications - Practical reference covering Minnesota's climate zones, frost dates, soil types, and seasonal growing conditions for native and cultivated plants.

6. [Minnesota's Ecological Subsections](https://www.dnr.state.mn.us/ecs/index.html) - Minnesota DNR - Official state classification of Minnesota's ecological subsections with maps, climate data, and descriptions of landform and vegetation patterns.

7. [USDA Plant Hardiness Zone Map](https://planthardiness.ars.usda.gov/) - USDA Agricultural Research Service - Interactive map showing Minnesota's hardiness zones based on average annual minimum temperatures, essential for selecting plants suited to local conditions.

8. [Minnesota Climatology](https://extension.umn.edu/weather-and-climate) - University of Minnesota Extension - Research-based climate data for Minnesota including precipitation patterns, growing degree days, and frost dates relevant to native plant establishment.

9. [Web Soil Survey](https://websoilsurvey.nrcs.usda.gov/) - USDA Natural Resources Conservation Service - Free tool for exploring detailed soil maps and data for any Minnesota location, helping gardeners match native plants to their specific soil conditions.

10. [Minnesota's Native Plant Communities](https://www.dnr.state.mn.us/npc/index.html) - Minnesota DNR - Classification system describing over 100 native plant community types found across Minnesota's ecoregions, linking vegetation patterns to environmental conditions.

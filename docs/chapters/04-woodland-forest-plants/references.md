# References: Woodland and Forest Plants

1. [Eastern Deciduous Forest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_deciduous_forests) - Wikipedia - Describes the temperate deciduous forest biome that covers southeastern Minnesota, including dominant tree species, understory layers, and seasonal dynamics.

2. [Boreal Forest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boreal_forest_of_Canada) - Wikipedia - Covers the boreal coniferous forest that extends into northern Minnesota, detailing spruce-fir communities, bog habitats, and cold-climate plant adaptations.

3. [Trillium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trillium) - Wikipedia - Profiles the iconic woodland wildflower genus found across Minnesota's forests, covering species identification, spring ephemeral ecology, and conservation status.

4. Tekiela, Stan - Trees of Minnesota Field Guide - Adventure Publications - Pocket-sized identification guide to Minnesota's native and common trees organized by leaf type, with range maps and habitat descriptions for each species.

5. Handel, Steven N. and Tallamy, Douglas W. - The Living Landscape - Timber Press - Demonstrates how to design landscapes using native woodland plants arranged in ecological communities, bridging forest ecology with practical garden design.

6. [Forests of Minnesota](https://www.dnr.state.mn.us/forests/index.html) - Minnesota DNR - State resource covering Minnesota's major forest types, tree species distributions, and forest management practices across the state's woodland regions.

7. [Woodland Wildflowers](https://www.minnesotawildflowers.info/woodland) - MinnesotaWildflowers.info - Photo-rich guide to Minnesota's native woodland wildflowers including spring ephemerals, ferns, and shade-tolerant species with detailed identification information.

8. [Native Trees and Shrubs](https://extension.umn.edu/find-plants/trees-and-shrubs) - University of Minnesota Extension - Research-based profiles of native trees and shrubs suited to Minnesota landscapes, with planting guidance, pest information, and site requirements.

9. [Minnesota Old-Growth Forests](https://www.dnr.state.mn.us/forests/oldgrowth/index.html) - Minnesota DNR - Documents Minnesota's remaining old-growth forest stands, describing their ecological characteristics and the rare plant communities they support.

10. [Lady Bird Johnson Wildflower Center](https://www.wildflower.org/plants) - University of Texas at Austin - National native plant database with species profiles, range maps, and cultivation guides searchable by state, useful for confirming Minnesota woodland species.

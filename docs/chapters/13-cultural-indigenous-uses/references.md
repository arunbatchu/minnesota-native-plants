# References: Cultural and Indigenous Plant Uses

## Wikipedia

1. [Ethnobotany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnobotany) - Wikipedia - Provides foundational overview of how Indigenous peoples use plants for food, medicine, and ceremony, establishing the disciplinary framework for this chapter.

2. [Ojibwe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ojibwe) - Wikipedia - Covers the history, culture, and territorial range of the Ojibwe people in Minnesota, including their deep relationship with wild rice and forest plants.

3. [Dakota People](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dakota_people) - Wikipedia - Describes the Dakota nations of Minnesota, their cultural practices, and historical connections to prairie and woodland plant communities across the upper Midwest.

## Textbooks

4. Kindscher, Kelly. *Medicinal Wild Plants of the Prairie: An Ethnobotanical Guide* - University Press of Kansas - Documents traditional medicinal uses of over 200 prairie plant species by Indigenous peoples of the Great Plains, with detailed botanical descriptions.

5. Densmore, Frances. *How Indians Use Wild Plants for Food, Medicine, and Crafts* - Dover Publications - A classic ethnobotanical study originally conducted with Ojibwe communities in Minnesota, recording plant preparation methods, harvesting seasons, and cultural significance.

## Online Resources

6. [Traditional Ecological Knowledge and Natural Resource Management](https://www.mnhs.org/preservation/native-american) - Minnesota Historical Society - Offers archival materials documenting Indigenous land management practices and plant use in Minnesota from historical and contemporary perspectives.

7. [Tribal Nations in Minnesota](https://mn.gov/indianaffairs/tribal-nations.html) - Minnesota Indian Affairs Council - Provides official information on the eleven tribal nations in Minnesota, supporting respectful and accurate representation of Indigenous communities.

8. [Native American Ethnobotany Database](http://naeb.brit.org/) - University of Michigan / BRIT - A searchable database of over 44,000 entries documenting how Native American peoples use plants for food, medicine, fiber, and dye across North America.

9. [Honor the Earth](https://www.honorearth.org/) - Honor the Earth - Founded by Winona LaDuke, this organization highlights Ojibwe environmental advocacy and Indigenous land stewardship practices in northern Minnesota.

10. [Wild Rice and the Ojibwe People](https://www.1854treatyauthority.org/wildrice.html) - 1854 Treaty Authority - Explains the cultural and ecological significance of wild rice (manoomin) harvesting to Ojibwe communities in northeastern Minnesota treaty territories.

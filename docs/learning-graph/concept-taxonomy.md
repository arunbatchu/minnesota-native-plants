# Concept Taxonomy

The following taxonomy organizes the 288 concepts into 12 categories for visualization and navigation.

## Categories

### 1. Foundation Concepts (FOUND)

Core definitions, botany fundamentals, and prerequisite knowledge that all other concepts build upon.

- Concepts 1-10

### 2. Ecoregions and Geography (ECOR)

Minnesota's ecoregions, hardiness zones, soil types, and geographic context.

- Concepts 11-20

### 3. Prairie Ecosystem (PRAI)

Prairie plants, grasses, wildflowers, fire ecology, and grassland ecosystem concepts.

- Concepts 21-45

### 4. Woodland Ecosystem (WOOD)

Forest and woodland plants, trees, shrubs, spring ephemerals, and forest structure.

- Concepts 46-69

### 5. Wetland Ecosystem (WETL)

Wetland, marsh, bog, and shoreline plants plus water management functions.

- Concepts 70-86

### 6. Pollinators and Wildlife (POLL)

Pollinator species, wildlife habitat, bloom timing, and ecological relationships.

- Concepts 87-103

### 7. Invasive Species (INVA)

Invasive species identification, impacts, removal methods, and management strategies.

- Concepts 104-133

### 8. Plant Identification (PLID)

Skills and tools for identifying native and invasive plants in the field.

- Concepts 134-144

### 9. Garden Design and Maintenance (GARD)

Site assessment, garden design, planting techniques, maintenance, and sourcing.

- Concepts 145-186

### 10. Ecological Restoration (REST)

Restoration ecology principles, methods, monitoring, and adaptive management.

- Concepts 187-206

### 11. Cultural and Indigenous Uses (CULT)

Ethnobotany, traditional plant knowledge, indigenous land management, and cultural significance.

- Concepts 207-215

### 12. Minnesota Resources (MNRS)

State, county, city, university, and nonprofit programs and organizations.

- Concepts 216-240

### 13. Systems Thinking (SYST)

Ecosystem interconnections, feedback loops, trophic cascades, and systems analysis.

- Concepts 241-258

### 14. Critical Thinking and Misinformation (CRIT)

Evidence-based practices, misinformation detection, fact-checking, and logical reasoning.

- Concepts 259-280

### 15. Capstone Projects (CAPS)

Culminating projects that synthesize multiple concept areas.

- Concepts 281-288

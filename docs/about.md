## What Is This Book?

**Minnesota Native Plants** is a free, interactive intelligent textbook designed to help anyone learn about the native plants, ecosystems, and ecological principles of Minnesota. Whether you are a home gardener, a student, a restoration volunteer, or simply curious about the natural world around you, this book is for you.

## Who Is It For?

This textbook is written for a general public audience with no prior botany or ecology background required. We use plain language, define technical terms on first use, and provide interactive simulations to help you build intuition about ecological concepts.

## What Makes It "Intelligent"?

This is not a traditional textbook. It includes several features that make learning more engaging and effective:

- **Interactive MicroSims** — hands-on simulations that let you explore concepts like fire ecology, pollinator matching, and garden design
- **Learning Graph** — a structured map of 289 interconnected concepts showing how ideas build on each other
- **Per-Chapter Quizzes** — test your understanding with 10 questions per chapter
- **Pedagogical Agent** — Bree the Bee guides you through content with tips, encouragement, and key insights
- **Mermaid Diagrams** — visual flowcharts and concept maps embedded throughout

## How Is It Organized?

The book is organized into 17 chapters that progress from foundational ecology through plant identification, garden design, ecological restoration, and systems thinking:

1. **Chapters 1–2** — Ecological foundations and Minnesota's ecoregions
2. **Chapters 3–5** — Plant communities: prairies, woodlands, and wetlands
3. **Chapters 6–9** — Wildlife interactions and invasive species
4. **Chapters 10–11** — Garden design and planting practices
5. **Chapters 12–14** — Restoration, cultural uses, and Minnesota resources
6. **Chapters 15–17** — Systems thinking, critical thinking, and capstone projects
